# Calculus scratchpad

The fundamental theorem ties the derivative to the integral: for a continuous $f$ on $[a,b]$,

$$
\int_a^b f'(x)\,dx = f(b) - f(a)
$$

A Gaussian integral for good measure, with its inline cousin $\sigma^2$:

$$
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2}\,dx = \sqrt{\pi}
$$

And a series that converges to $\frac{\pi^2}{6}$:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6}
$$
